---
title: Social Security Benefits
description: How to set up Social Security estimates in your scenario
---

Net Worth Navigator does **not** have a built-in Social Security calculation engine. You provide your own benefit estimates, which you can get directly from the Social Security Administration.

## Getting Your Estimates

Visit **[ssa.gov/myaccount](https://www.ssa.gov/myaccount/)** and log in to your Social Security account. Once inside:

1. Go to the **"Estimate Your Future Benefits"** section
2. Review your estimated monthly benefit at **full retirement age** (67 for most people)
3. The SSA also provides estimates for early retirement (age 62) and delayed retirement (age 70)

For the most accurate modeling, you'll want estimates at **each age from 62 to 70**. The SSA calculator may let you enter a specific claim age to see the adjusted amount — enter each age and record the monthly benefit.

## Entering Benefits in Your Scenario

Once you have your estimates, open your scenario TOML file (or use the **Raw TOML** tab in the Setup Panel). Find the `[person1]` section and add a `social_security_benefits` sub-table:

```toml
# Social Security timing
ss_start_age = 67   # the age you plan to claim

[person1.social_security_benefits]
"62" = 1700   # estimated monthly benefit if claimed at 62
"63" = 1840
"64" = 1990
"65" = 2130
"66" = 2280
"67" = 2440   # full retirement age benefit
"68" = 2640
"69" = 2840
"70" = 3080   # maximum benefit (delayed credits stop here)
```

Do the same for `[person2]` if you're modeling a couple:

```toml
[person2.social_security_benefits]
"62" = 1100
"63" = 1180
"64" = 1270
"65" = 1360
"66" = 1460
"67" = 1550
"68" = 1670
"69" = 1800
"70" = 1950
```

## How the Model Uses This Data

- **`ss_start_age`** — the age at which Social Security payments begin for that person. This is the **source of truth** for SS timing. The model auto-synthesizes a `SocialSecurity` event from this setting — you don't need to write one manually.

- **`social_security_benefits`** — a table mapping each claim age (62–70) to the estimated monthly benefit. The model looks up the benefit amount that matches your chosen `ss_start_age`. If `ss_start_age` is 67, it uses `"67" = 2440`.

- **Widow/er step-up** — when one partner passes away (end-of-plan year), the survivor steps up to the deceased partner's benefit if it is higher. The survivor's own benefit is replaced starting at `survivor_ss_start_age` (default: 60).

## Starter Templates

The `starter.toml` and `starter-couple.toml` files include a commented guide for Social Security:

```toml
# Social Security benefit by claim age (get your estimate at ssa.gov/myaccount)
[person1.social_security_benefits]
67 = 0   # ← YOUR VALUE: estimated monthly benefit at your claim age
```

The sample scenarios (`sample.toml`, `sample-couples.toml`, `sample-a.toml`, `sample-b.toml`) include fully populated example benefit tables — look at them for reference if you're unsure about the format.

## What the Model Does NOT Do

Net Worth Navigator does **not** calculate or adjust Social Security benefits. It does not:

- Apply the Social Security earnings test or bend-point formulas
- Model spousal or divorced-spouse benefits
- Apply WEP (Windfall Elimination Provision) or GPO (Government Pension Offset)
- Adjust benefits for early/late retirement beyond the estimates you provide
- Predict COLAs (cost-of-living adjustments) — use today's dollars and let the model's inflation setting handle purchasing power

For any of the above, consult the [Social Security Administration](https://www.ssa.gov/benefits/retirement/planner.html) or a financial planner.
