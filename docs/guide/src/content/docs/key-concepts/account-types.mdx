Net Worth Navigator classifies your accounts into five buckets. Each bucket has different tax treatment, growth characteristics, and behavior in the simulation.

See the [Manual Entry](/Net-Worth-Navigator/data-sources/manual-entry/) page for an annotated screenshot of where these buckets appear in the Setup Panel.

## The Five Buckets
### Cash

**What it is:** Checking accounts, savings accounts, money market accounts, and any other liquid cash holdings.

**Model behavior:** Cash earns a separate return (typically lower than the blended investable return). It's the first source for spending needs, and surplus routing refills it to the phase target before investing.

**Tax treatment:** Interest is taxable as ordinary income, but simplified in the model.

### Taxable Brokerage

**What it is:** Regular investment accounts — not tax-advantaged retirement accounts. Includes standard brokerage accounts and joint investment accounts.

**Model behavior:** Subject to capital gains taxation on withdrawals (simplified as a taxable fraction). Grows at the blended stock/bond return rate.

**Tax treatment:** Withdrawals trigger taxable income for the gain portion. The model tracks a cost-basis ledger.

### Traditional IRA / 401(k)

**What it is:** Pre-tax retirement accounts. Traditional IRA, traditional 401(k), and similar tax-deferred accounts.

**Model behavior:** Contributions reduce taxable income in the contribution year. Withdrawals in retirement are taxed as ordinary income. Subject to Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs) starting at age 73.

**Tax treatment:** Full withdrawal amount is taxable as ordinary income. This is typically the largest source of taxable income in retirement.

### Roth IRA / 401(k)

**What it is:** After-tax retirement accounts. Roth IRA, Roth 401(k), and similar accounts.

**Model behavior:** Contributions are made with after-tax money. Contributions (not earnings) can be withdrawn at any time without penalty or tax. Qualified withdrawals in retirement are tax-free.

**Tax treatment:** No tax on qualified withdrawals. The model tracks contribution basis separately from earnings.

### Home Equity

**What it is:** Home value minus mortgage balance. Tracked through the `home_value` field and liability balances.

**Model behavior:** Home value grows at the inflation rate (or a configurable appreciation rate). A home's equity is included in net worth but is not available for liquid spending — the model does not assume you'll sell your home to fund retirement unless a `SellHome` event is configured.

**Tax treatment:** Home equity growth is not taxed in the model (principal residence capital gains exclusion is standard).

## Owner Attribution

For couples, retirement accounts (traditional IRA and Roth) can be attributed to each person:

- **Person 1's traditional IRA/401(k)** → taxed on that person's return
- **Person 2's traditional IRA/401(k)** → taxed on that person's return
- **Person 1's Roth IRA** → tax-free, attributed to that person
- **Person 2's Roth IRA** → tax-free, attributed to that person

Owner attribution matters for RMD calculations, Social Security taxation, and survivor-phase planning. The Accounts and Portfolio tabs in the projection show per-person breakdowns.

## The Bucket Approach in Practice

When using [Manual Entry](/Net-Worth-Navigator/data-sources/manual-entry/), you add up the balances in each bucket and enter the totals:

- Add up all non-retirement investment accounts → **Taxable brokerage**
- Add up all pre-tax retirement accounts → **Traditional IRA / 401(k)**
- Add up all after-tax retirement accounts → **Roth IRA / 401(k)**
- Add up all cash accounts → **Cash**
- Enter your estimated home value → **Home Value**
