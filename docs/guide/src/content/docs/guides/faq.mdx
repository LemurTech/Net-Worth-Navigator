<details>
<summary><strong>Does NWN account for inflation?</strong></summary>

**Yes.** Net Worth Navigator models inflation in three integrated ways:

**Portfolio growth uses nominal returns.** You set `stock_return`, `bond_return`, and `equity_allocation` in your scenario's `[assumptions]` section. The model compounds at the full nominal rate — no automatic inflation deduction from returns. Cash buckets grow at a separate `cash_return` rate (defaults to `inflation` if not set).

**Spending targets CPI-escalate each year.** By default (`spending_basis = "real"`), your `retirement_annual` and `pre_retirement_spending` targets are expressed in today's dollars and grown forward by the `inflation` rate each year. For example, a $75,000 annual spend target becomes ~$100,800 after 10 years at 3% inflation — the portfolio must support that, not a static figure. You can flip to `spending_basis = "nominal"` for fixed-dollar spending that does not inflate.

**Income grows at inflation plus real raise.** Take-home income compounds as `(1 + inflation) × (1 + real_raise) - 1`. So 3% inflation plus a 2% real raise produces ~5.06% nominal income growth per year. Configured via `annual_take_home_real_raise` per person.

The net effect is equivalent to using real returns, but more granular — spending, income, cash returns, and home appreciation each inflate at their own rate.

**Real-dollar display:** Set `[simulation].real_dollar_basis = true` in your scenario to see all chart and table figures deflated to start-year purchasing power (for example, values in 2026 dollars when your scenario starts in 2026). When enabled, a segmented toggle pill (💰 Real | 📊 Nominal) appears in the value-basis bar below the KPI numbers, allowing instant switching between inflation-adjusted and nominal views. Your preference is remembered across page reloads. Default is `false` (nominal future-dollar display).
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>What data source should I use?</strong></summary>

It depends on your situation:

- **Manual Entry (synthetic):** Best for first-time users and what-if exploration. You enter high-level bucket balances directly (taxable, traditional IRA, Roth, cash). No external account setup required.
- **Monarch Money (live sync):** Best if you already use Monarch for account tracking. Balances are fetched automatically each run via the Monarch MCP server.
- **CSV Import:** Best if you want account-level granularity without a Monarch subscription. Export a CSV from your financial institution and upload it.

See the [Data Sources](/Net-Worth-Navigator/data-sources/) section for detailed setup instructions.
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Can I use NWN without Monarch Money?</strong></summary>

**Yes.** Monarch is entirely optional. Set `[data_source].mode = "synthetic"` in your scenario TOML (or select Manual Entry in the Setup Panel), and supply starting balances directly. NWN works fully offline with no external dependencies.
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>What do the different render modes mean?</strong></summary>

- **Deterministic:** Uses the exact return assumptions you configure. One single projection path. Best for most day-to-day planning.
- **Monte Carlo:** Runs hundreds of projections with randomly varied annual returns based on your mean return and volatility setting. Shows probability bands (10th–90th percentile) and a success rate.
- **Historical:** Applies actual market return sequences from past decades to your projection. Shows how your plan would have fared in different historical periods.

See [Render Modes](/Net-Worth-Navigator/key-concepts/render-modes/) for the full explanation.
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Is my financial data sent anywhere?</strong></summary>

**No.** Net Worth Navigator runs entirely on your local machine. All financial data stays in local files on your computer. The only network calls are optional (Monarch Money API if you choose live sync, and git when you update the application). See the [License & Security](/Net-Worth-Navigator/reference/license/) page for details.
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Can I compare different scenarios side by side?</strong></summary>

**Yes.** The [Compare Page](/Net-Worth-Navigator/guides/comparing-scenarios/) lets you select any two scenarios and view overlay charts, KPI deltas (difference in net worth, retirement age, success rate, etc.), and side-by-side parameter diffs.
</details>
